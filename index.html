About Me

Meredith Black is a former elementary school teacher looking to break into the field of web development. After spending over half a decade working in public education, Meredith knows how to actively listen to a variety of clients needs and apply complex problem solving and critical thinking to meet the needs of clients. Meredith practices professionalism by embracing best and safe practices and using data to help make decisions in order accomplish goals.


Meredith has garnered a variety of skills through her background experience in education. Meredith has displayed leadership and presentation skills by participating as grade level team leader at campus level meetings and events and presenting topics to fellow educators at the campus and district level. Meredith has displayed exceptional communication skills across various forms of media in order to be accessible to students, parents, and school officials. Meredith is known for her willingness to take on a challenge and use complex problem solving and critical thinking to tackle the task at hand.


Meredith is currently enrolled in the Coding Bootcamp at The University of Texas at Austin. Meredith also holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology from The University of Texas at San Antonio and is certified to teach Special Education: Early Childhood-12th Grade, General Education: Early Childhood-6th Grade, and English as a Second Language: Early Childhood-12th Grade.
